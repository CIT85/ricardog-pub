Chest Workouts

Bench Press

About bench pressing

Bench pressing is a popular strength-training exercise where you lie on a bench and lift a barbell or dumbbells using your arms. The movement involves lowering the weights down towards your chest and then pushing them back up, straightening your arms. It mainly works out your chest, shoulders, and arms, helping to build upper body strength and muscle. How To Bench Press:

  1. Lie flat on a bench with your feet planted firmly on the ground.
  2. Grip the barbell slightly wider that shoulder-width apart, lower it to your chest, and keep your elbows at around a 45-degree angle.
  3. Push the barbell upward by extending your arms, exhaling as you lift. Keep your back flat against the bench.
  4. Lower the barbell back to your chest in a controlled manner while inhaling.

Back Workouts

Barbell Rows

About barbell rows

Barbell rows are a type of strength training exercise that primarily targets the muscles in your upper back. How To Perform A Barbell Row:

  1. Start by standing with your feet shoulder-width apart, knees slightly bent, and grip a barbell with an overhand grip (palms facing down) at about shoulder width.
  2. Bend at your hips and slightly at the knees while keeping your back straight. Your torso should be almost parallel to the ground, and your arms should be fully extended toward the floor.
  3. Keeping your core engaged, pull the barbell towards your lower chest by retracting your shoulder blades. Keep your elbows close to your body as you lift the weight.
  4. Squeeze your back muscles at the top of the movement, then slowly lower the barbell back to the starting position in a controlled manner.

Leg Workouts

Squats

About squats

Squats are a type of exercise where you bend your knees, lower your hips, and lower your body toward the ground while keeping your back straight. It's like sitting down on an imaginary chair and then standing back up. Squats work various muscles in your legs, including your thighs, hamstrings, and glutes, and they're great for building strength and improving overall lower body fitness. How To Squat:

  1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, toes slightly turned out.
  2. Keep your back straight and chest up. Engage your core.
  3. Bend your knees and hips, lowering your body as if sitting back into an imaginary chair.
  4. Lower yourself until your thighs are parallel to the ground or slightly below.
  5. Push through your heels and extend your legs to return to the starting position.

Forming Effective Workout Routines

Introduction

Knowing the excercises is only a small part of what it takes to accomplish what your setting out to do in the gym. Knowing is only half the battle, next we need to put some thought into how and when we're performing these excercises.

Fitness Goals

Depending on what your trying to accomplish, the order in which you perform these excercises, along with your dieting, will be crucial in maximizing your success. It is important to understand what you are trying to accomplish, are you trying to lose body fat, or perhaps you are looking to increase your strength. Maybe you just want to put on muscle and size and are not really concerned with having the biggest lift. All of these factor in to how you may approach your activities in the gym.
